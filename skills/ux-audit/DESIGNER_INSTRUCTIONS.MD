## How to run the audit

1. Confirm the exact product area or workflow you are auditing.
   - Do not audit the whole product.
   - Pick one bounded slice that can be reviewed end to end.

2. Gather the starting context.
   - Open the product or artifact links in this ticket.
   - Review any listed past projects, known pain points, and prior notes.
   - If access or scope is unclear, resolve that before continuing.

3. Run the UX audit workflow.
   - Use the `/ux-audit` skill for the chosen product slice.
   - Walk the workflow as a real user would.
   - Capture screenshots and concrete examples of friction as you go.
   - Focus on evidence, not generic opinions.

4. Look for the strongest opportunity areas.
   Pay special attention to:
   - confusing hierarchy,
   - unclear next steps,
   - unnecessary effort,
   - weak or missing feedback,
   - inconsistent patterns,
   - trust issues,
   - places where the experience may be creating avoidable support, sales, or adoption friction.

5. Synthesize the findings.
   - Group observations into 3–5 themes.
   - Separate quick wins from larger opportunities.
   - Identify which issue appears most valuable to solve first.
   - Write a short explanation of why the client should care.

6. Package the output.
   Produce:
   - the finished audit report,
   - supporting screenshots or references,
   - a short summary of the main opportunity,
   - a recommendation: `Park`, `Pursue`, or `Graduate to dedicated project`.

   If the work should become a durable branded document, hand off to `paged-document` after findings are reviewed and prioritized. Use [references/paged-document-handoff.md](references/paged-document-handoff.md) to gather the source packet, start the outline, and carry the work through RoleModel voice review, layout QA, and PDF export.

7. Close the task with a clear next move.
   - Add all artifact links to this ticket.
   - State what should happen next and who should own it.
   - If the opportunity is large enough to become real work, recommend or create a separate follow-on project instead of expanding this task indefinitely.
