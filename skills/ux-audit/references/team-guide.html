Why We're Doing This

Show up with
energy,
not a list of
problems.

We have a real opportunity with long-standing partners — products we may have launched years ago that could genuinely benefit from what we can do today.

The goal

Not to walk in with a list of problems — but with a compelling vision for what these products could become.

For partners

These audits should feel exciting, not critical. An honest look at where things stand and a motivating picture of what's possible.

For us

The AI-accelerated workflow compresses over a week of work into 2–3 focused days. That's how we make this practice scale.

What We're Evaluating

Four lenses.
Every product, every time.

01
Experience Gaps
Moments where users have to work harder than they should. Confusing navigation, flows that don't match how people think about a task, forms that ask for too much.
Where does this product fight the user?
02
Visual & Brand Coherence
Design inconsistency accumulates quietly. Different button treatments, mismatched type scales, color drift — these erode trust even when users can't articulate why.
Does this feel like one product?
03
Modernization Moments
High-visibility improvements that have become table stakes since the original design: accessibility compliance, mobile responsiveness, a proper component system.
What has the industry moved past us on?
04
Strategic Opportunities
Where is the product constrained by old decisions that are actually solvable now? What could it do that it doesn't today? This section should feel generative — a glimpse of v-next.
What becomes possible next?
The 2–3 Day Process

Structured, fast, and repeatable.

Day 1
Discover & Document
Run a structured heuristic walkthrough using the audit checklist. Use Claude to surface friction points, accessibility gaps, and pattern inconsistencies. Pull existing Figma files — use the Figma MCP to extract tokens, component inventories, and layout patterns.
Producing:
Annotated findings list · Component inventory · First-pass friction map
Day 2
Synthesize & Prioritize
Not every finding is worth presenting. Prioritize on two axes: user impact and implementation effort. Claude helps draft the narrative framing. By end of day, rough wireframes in Figma for the 2–3 highest-impact opportunities.
Producing:
Prioritized findings · Draft narrative · Initial wireframes
Day 3
Prototype & Package
Pick one or two of the strongest opportunities and build a lightweight vibe-coded prototype. One flow, done well. The goal is an "aha" moment. Then assemble the deliverable — Claude drafts narrative, Figma handles visual presentation.
Producing:
Clickable prototype · Complete partner-facing deliverable
How We Present It

The "Then / Now / Next" frame.

Then
Honor the original work
We honor the original work and the context it was built in. What were the constraints? What did it accomplish? We say this genuinely — not as diplomatic setup, but because it's true and it matters.
"This was well-executed for the context it was built in."
Now
Name what's changed
We name what's changed: user expectations, technology, the competitive landscape. This isn't criticism — it's context. Framed honestly, partners recognize it immediately and feel respected.
"When this was built, X — today users expect Y."
Next
The opportunity
This is where the energy goes. The wireframes, the prototype, the invitation. We're starting a conversation, not closing a sale. What could phase one look like? What would we explore together?
"Here's what becomes possible."
Deliverable Structure

The output should feel like
a magazine, not a bug report.

01
Executive Summary
One page, shareable upward. Plain language, no design jargon. What's the core opportunity, what are the 2–3 things we'd do, and what's the rough shape of the impact.
02
Findings
Organized by theme, not severity. Each finding pairs what we observed with what this means for users. Annotated screenshots throughout. Grounded in behavior, not opinion.
03
Opportunities
Wireframes, UI design sketches, and the prototype. Visually rich, relatively concise. We're showing a direction and making people lean forward — not justifying every decision.
04
What's Next
An explicit invitation. What could phase one look like? What questions would we want to explore together? Leave it open — we're starting a conversation, not closing a sale.
Running This as a Repeatable Practice

What we standardize
across every engagement.

01
Run the Day 1 checklist — it's the consistent foundation that makes every audit start fast
02
Use Claude and the Figma MCP throughout — they compress a week of cataloging into hours
03

04
Package the deliverable so it earns a yes — magazine, not bug report
